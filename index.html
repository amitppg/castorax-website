What is pharmaceutical grade castor oil?

+

Pharmaceutical grade castor oil is a highly purified oil that meets USP (United States Pharmacopeia), BP (British Pharmacopeia), and IP (Indian Pharmacopeia) standards. It is cold-pressed, hexane-free, and contains approximately 90% ricinoleic acid. Used as an excipient in drug formulations, laxatives, and topical preparations. We provide complete COA and SDS documentation with every batch.

What is the difference between USP, BP, and IP castor oil?

+

USP (United States Pharmacopeia), BP (British Pharmacopeia), and IP (Indian Pharmacopeia) are international quality standards for pharmaceutical ingredients. All three have similar specifications for castor oil including purity, refractive index, specific gravity, and acid value. The main difference is the regulatory body - USP for USA, BP for UK/Europe, and IP for India. Our pharmaceutical grade castor oil meets all three standards, making it suitable for export to any market.

What is the shelf life of castor oil?

+

Pharmaceutical and cosmetic grade castor oil typically has a shelf life of 18-24 months when stored properly in sealed containers at temperatures below 25¬∞C, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Industrial grades may have longer shelf life. We provide specific shelf life information, manufacturing date, and storage recommendations with each product shipment.

What is hydrogenated castor oil used for?

+

Hydrogenated castor oil (also called castor wax) is a hard, white waxy solid with a melting point of 85-88¬∞C. It is used in pharmaceutical applications as a tablet coating agent, controlled-release agent, and tablet lubricant. In cosmetics, it's used in lipsticks, lip balms, deodorant sticks, hair pomades, and styling waxes. It provides excellent stability, non-greasy texture, and is a vegetable-based alternative to petroleum waxes.

How do I verify castor oil quality?

+

Verify castor oil quality through: (1) Request Certificate of Analysis (COA) showing test results for specific gravity (0.956-0.968), refractive index (1.477-1.481), acid value (max 2.0), and iodine value (82-90). (2) Check supplier credentials - Drug License, Export License, GMP certification. (3) Request sample for independent testing. (4) Verify batch-to-batch consistency. (5) Ensure complete traceability from source. We provide complete testing reports and third-party verification available upon request.
